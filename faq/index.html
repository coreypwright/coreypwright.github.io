Frequently Asked Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Last updated: March 31, 2020

Q: What do I need to get started?

A: All you need is an external microphone, software that allows you to transmit audio such as Skype, and an internet-ready device like a laptop, tablet, or smart phone on which to view the captions.

Q: Can you transcribe math courses?

A: Yes. TypeWell includes “math mode” which allows the transcriber to rapidly render mathematical symbols and equations.

Q: Can I see how this works before I make a request?

A: Yes. Please contact me if you would like to see a brief demo.

Q: What is “meaning-for-meaning”?

A: This concept refers to the idea of summarizing verbal content in a way that faithfully preserves the speaker’s intended meaning. This goes hand in hand with the idea that there are certain components of speech that do not contribute to the overall meaning of the utterance, such as false starts, stuttering, or repetition, that ought to be omitted to enhance the readability of the captions. For highly technical courses, however, heavy summarization is not ideal unless the captionist has expert proficiency in that subject.

Q: What is “near-verbatim”?

A: Near verbatim means that, to the best of my ability, I type virtually every utterance that is made. The benefit is that this does not rely on the captionist’s ability to make effective and faithful summarizations of content. Thus, I only make extremely minimal modifications or omissions. The nature of the omissions will often depend on the speaker’s habits and overall pace of speech.

Q: Do you do CART?

A: Communication access real-time translation (CART) is a blanket term that essentially means “real-time captioning”. In practice, CART carries a more specific meaning that usually refers to verbatim (word-for-word) captioning using stenographic techniques. As I am not a stenographer, I cannot be certified by the National Court Reporters Association (NCRA) as a CART provider. However, if the captionist can type quickly enough to provide verbatim/near-verbatim captioning, it makes little difference to the end-user whether stenography or other abbreviation methods are used. There is functionally no difference between my near-verbatim service and what is traditionally considered CART.
